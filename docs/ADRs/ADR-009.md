# ADR-009: GNN Clustering Architecture (GraphSAGE on HNSW Graphs)

- **Status**: Proposed
- **Date**: 2026-03-23
- **Context**: The plan proposes constructing an email graph from HNSW neighbor relationships and applying GraphSAGE for topic discovery. The research paper notes this is a novel contribution but flags that HNSW graphs are optimized for navigability, not semantic coherence -- empirical validation is needed.
- **Decision**: Use GraphSAGE on HNSW neighbor graphs with stability guardrails and fallback to simpler clustering.
- **Consequences**: GNN adds computational cost (~8s for full re-clustering of 100K emails). Stability guardrails prevent user confusion but may delay discovery of new topics. Fallback to simpler methods ensures graceful degradation if GNN quality is poor.
- **Alternatives Considered**: K-means on raw vectors (simpler, no GNN needed, but misses graph structure), Louvain community detection (works on communication graph, misses content), LDA topic modeling (probabilistic, well-understood, but not vector-native).
- **Research References**: Hamilton et al. 2017 (GraphSAGE: Inductive Representation Learning on Large Graphs); HNSW graph navigability properties; HDBSCAN density-based clustering.

## Detailed Design

### Graph Construction

1. Vertices = emails, Edges = HNSW k-nearest neighbors (k=20).
2. Edge weight = cosine similarity between email vectors.
3. Additional edges: same-sender edges (weight 0.3), same-thread edges (weight 0.8).
4. This creates a multi-relational graph combining content similarity and communication structure.

### GraphSAGE Configuration

- 2-layer GNN, mean aggregation, hidden dimension 128.
- Sampling: 10 neighbors at layer 1, 5 at layer 2.
- Unsupervised training: node proximity loss (nearby nodes should have similar embeddings).

### Clustering on GNN Embeddings

- **Primary**: HDBSCAN (density-based, finds natural cluster count).
- **Fallback**: Mini-batch K-means if HDBSCAN produces too many noise points (>30% of emails unassigned).

### Stability Guardrails

These mechanisms prevent cluster churn between runs, which would confuse users:

1. **Hysteresis**: An email must be closer to a new cluster centroid by margin delta=0.05 before reassignment.
2. **Pinning**: User-renamed or user-pinned clusters are never auto-dissolved.
3. **Minimum age**: Clusters must persist for 3 consecutive re-clustering runs before being shown to the user.
4. **Merge threshold**: Clusters with centroid similarity > 0.85 are candidates for merge (user approval required).
5. **Split detection**: If cluster variance exceeds 2x the corpus mean, suggest a split to the user.

### Incremental Update Schedule

| Trigger | Action | Cost |
|---------|--------|------|
| New email arrives | Assign to nearest cluster centroid (O(1) lookup) | Negligible |
| Hourly | Incremental re-clustering (mini-batch K-means on recent emails) | Low |
| Daily | Full GraphSAGE + HDBSCAN re-clustering | ~8s for 100K emails |

### Validation Strategy

Compare GraphSAGE clusters against:
- (a) Plain K-means on raw vectors.
- (b) HDBSCAN on raw vectors (no GNN).

GraphSAGE should show a higher silhouette coefficient and better Adjusted Rand Index (ARI) against human labels. If it does not outperform simpler methods, fall back to HDBSCAN on raw vectors permanently and log the result for future analysis.

## Options Considered

### Option 1: K-Means on Raw Vectors
- **Pros**: Simple, well-understood, fast. No GNN dependency. Works well for globular clusters.
- **Cons**: Requires specifying K upfront. Misses graph structure (sender relationships, thread connections). Sensitive to outliers. Poor on non-spherical cluster shapes.

### Option 2: Louvain Community Detection
- **Pros**: Works directly on communication graphs. No embedding needed for graph structure. Finds natural community count.
- **Cons**: Optimizes for modularity on the communication graph, not content similarity. Two emails about different topics from the same sender would cluster together. Misses the semantic dimension.

### Option 3: LDA Topic Modeling
- **Pros**: Probabilistic model with well-understood theory. Each email can belong to multiple topics. Interpretable topic-word distributions.
- **Cons**: Bag-of-words assumption loses word order and context. Not vector-native -- requires separate text processing pipeline. Struggles with short texts (email subjects, brief replies).

### Option 4: GraphSAGE on HNSW Graphs with Guardrails (Selected)
- **Pros**: Combines content similarity (vector edges) with communication structure (sender/thread edges). Learns richer representations than raw vectors. HDBSCAN finds natural cluster count. Stability guardrails prevent user-facing churn.
- **Cons**: Most complex option. HNSW graphs are optimized for navigability, not semantic coherence (empirical validation required). GNN training adds computational cost. If validation fails, falls back to simpler methods anyway.
